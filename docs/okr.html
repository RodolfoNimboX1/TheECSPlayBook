OKR Framework

Our company objectives are based on a Framework known as OKR. In this article you can learn everything about it!

Anatomy of an OKR

Objective

Qualitative and Inspirational, Time Bound, and Actionable by the Team Independently (no interdependence!).

Some good objectives

  • Own the direct to business coffee retail market in the south bay.
  • Launch an Awesome MVP.
  • Close a round that lets us kill it next quarter.

Some poor objectives

  • Sales numbers up 30%.
  • Double users.
  • Raise a series B of 5M.

They are poor objectives because they are actually Key Results!

Key Results

Key results take all that inspirational language and quantifies it. It’s how you know if you achieved your objective. They are the metrics that change if we succeed. Typically you have only 3 Key Results per Objective.

Avoid setting Key Results as projects (tactics) 9, focus on the metrics you want to move. If a key result is a result, you can change your tactics until the numbers move. If a result is a project, you have locked yourself into achieving it, even if you find out it’s the wrong approach.

KRs should be difficult, not impossible. If you assigned a confidence level of completion to each KR from 1 to 10 (1 meaning “never gonna happen my friend”) at the beginning of the quarter, they should all be at 5/10. A 50/50 shot.
