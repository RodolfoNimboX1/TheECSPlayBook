Zoom Meetings

Even though we have specific guidelines for specific type of meetings is always good to know what we can do when we’re taking a regular meeting on zoom in the office.
On a space like ours where everything is shared and nothing is assigned its important to always be conscious of our surroundings.

How to have a successful zoom meeting...

without disrupting everyone around you:

  • Use a normal voice just because you can’t hear yourself doesn’t mean you need to scream, most of the time you can use your normal voice and if its too low people will probably ask you to speak a bit louder.
  • Careful with your headphones! Be them noise-cancelling or noise isolating, its important to remind yourself that you have something buffering between your ears and your voice, your voice is probably louder.

That being said there are a few type of scenarios that can happen on the daily, first it is important to differentiate:

I work on a laptop, not a desktop, so:

When focused it’s easy to forget what we’re all working on but on a call or zoom session is important to take notice. Making calls, meetings, sessions, etc, these all mean you’re going to make noise but… how many of the people around you can move if they needed silence (for their own reasons)?
Not everyone has a laptop and can move easily, if you’re on a predominantly desktop-full table maybe you should consider moving elsewhere be it a meeting room or other tables. This is mostly applicable if you notice these people are also on zoom sessions, they can’t do anything about location like you.

I work on a desktop:

Taking these meetings exactly where you are is a fact, the only option for when other people on laptops are making too much noise, should it be needed, ask them kindly to lower their voice or please take the call elsewhere.
Should the case be different people on different meetings on zoom sessions on desktop, be conscious between yourselves of the volume of your voice.

Taking calls with clients vs taking calls with coworkers

When taking calls with coworkers it might be less important if you can hear joking around, comments, etc in the background but taking calls with clients is very different. If you are sitting in a quiet space still surrounded by coworkers there might be no problem but if the people around you are talking loudly, singing happy birthday or just joking around for a couple of minutes it’s your responsibility to be prepared.
If you’re a laptop user, easily move to a less noiseful space, if on a desktop your options might be limited but asking your coworkers to keep it down for a couple of minutes might be the best answer, if not moving to a meeting room for the time you need might be best.
